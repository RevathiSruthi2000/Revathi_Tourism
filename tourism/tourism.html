The Golden Temple, also known as Harmandir Sahib, meaning "abode of God", is a gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar, Punjab, India.The gurdwara is built around a man-made pool (sarovar) that was completed by the fourth Sikh Guru, Guru Ram Das in 1577.

Varanasi Temple

Varanasi, one of the oldest cities in the world, is home to the iconic Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. This revered temple, situated on the banks of the Ganges River, is a significant pilgrimage site for Hindus and attracts millions of devotees each year. The temple's golden spire and intricate carvings reflect exquisite craftsmanship and devotion. Pilgrims come to offer prayers and perform rituals, believing that a visit here grants moksha, or liberation from the cycle of rebirth. The vibrant atmosphere of Varanasi, combined with its spiritual essence, makes it a unique and profound experience for visitors.
